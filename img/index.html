Biodegradable Polysaccharide-based Hydrogel in an Aluminum Battery

Background

Lithium-ion batteries are currently the de facto energy storage solution in numerous applications, as they possess high energy density and storage capacity. However, their chemical composition poses several physical limitations, as well as personal and environmental harm. Alternative solutions such as aluminum-air batteries have the potential to address the concerns with lithium-ion batteries, but come with their own limitations. For instance, aluminum-air batteries have a corrosive anode, resulting in irreversible byproducts that prevent further electrochemical reactions and preventing rechargeability.

Rationale

The potential of biodegradable/recyclable polysaccharides as electrolytes has not been explored. A successful battery design that implements this type of electrolyte could improve energy density, cost-efficiency, and safety. Fucoidan is a biodegradable polysaccharide with high electrical conductivity and ionic properties. However, its viability in a battery electrolyte was yet to be explored. The engineering goal of this research was to design, build, and test an aluminum battery containing a Fucoidan hydrogel, and compare its performance with batteries containing conventional ionic electrolytes.

Research Practicum

Research Practicum is a sophomore year course that marks the final chapter of required research at High Tech. For an entire semester, students are given time to meet with faculty mentors, conduct their experiments, and statistically analyze their results. Students then write a conclusive research paper, integrating their work from both freshman and sophomore years. Approximately 50% of the class then goes on to compete in various research competitions (please see Competitions for further information). In addition to research and data analysis skills, students will develop skills in communication, punctuality, time management, organization, decision making, resource allocation, ethical competition, and safety.
